Class 9 & 10

Know Your Stream First

🌐 What You Will Study
History, Geography, Political Science, Economics, Sociology, Psychology, Philosophy, English Literature — and more, depending on what you choose. Unlike Science or Commerce, Arts gives you the freedom to pick subjects based on what genuinely interests you. Every subject is about understanding human beings — their past, societies, minds, and choices.
⏱️ How Much You Need to Study
Expect 2–4 hours of focused self-study daily. But you need to actually read and think, not just scan notes. Arts rewards students who read widely, beyond just the textbook. Answer-writing and expression matter more here than speed or formula recall.
📖 The Big Difference from Class 10
In Class 10 Social Science, you touched on History, Geography, Civics, and Economics as one combined subject. In Class 11 Humanities, each becomes a full subject with a much deeper syllabus. You are expected to think critically, give structured arguments, and show genuine understanding — not just write points from memory.
💡 What Kind of Student Thrives Here
Loves reading and writing Curious about society and people Thinks in arguments, not formulas Expressive and articulate
⚠️ Honest Reality
Arts is not the "easy stream" — it is simply a different kind of hard. The syllabus is vast, and writing quality matters enormously in exams. Many students who do not enjoy reading struggle here despite studying for hours. Ignore the stereotype that Arts is for weak students. That is outdated and wrong.
☆ Opportunities Students Should Know Early

These are not mandatory — but students who do them early build a profile that opens doors others don't even know exist.

🛡️ NCC (National Cadet Corps)
NCC is especially powerful for Humanities students who aspire toward civil services, defence, or public administration. It builds the discipline, physical fitness, and leadership that UPSC boards and defence boards actively look for. An NCC C certificate can directly affect merit-based selections.
Civil services profile Defence advantage Leadership record
🏆 Regional / National Representation
Law schools, journalism institutes, and social science programs heavily weight extracurricular achievement. Representing your school in debate, sports, cultural events, or model conferences shows initiative. It also gives you real stories to tell in personal interviews and SOPs.
Law school edge SOP material Interview confidence
🗣️ Debate / MUN / Public Speaking
This is the single most valuable activity for a Humanities student. MUN teaches diplomatic negotiation, structured argumentation, and poise under pressure. Debate sharpens your thinking and writing. These are not soft hobbies — they are direct preparation for law, journalism, UPSC, and management.
UPSC prep Law interviews MUN leadership
🏛️ Social Science Projects & Cultural Competitions
Heritage club, history projects, school magazine editorials, art exhibitions — Humanities students have access to competitions that Science students often ignore. These build a creative, intellectual portfolio that premium colleges in social sciences and design actively seek.
Portfolio building Design college prep Intellectual profile
📝 Writing / Blogging / Digital Skills
Journalism, content creation, policy writing, and law are increasingly digital. Learning to write clearly and consistently — a blog, articles, or even social media writing on issues you care about — demonstrates passion and communication ability. Many journalism and media colleges ask for writing samples.

Class 11 & 12

What These Two Years Actually Look Like

💎 Subject Combinations
Arts gives you the most flexibility of any stream. You choose 5 subjects from a wide menu:
  • Common Core Subjects History, Geography, Political Science, Economics, English Core. Most students take 3–4 of these as their main subjects.
  • Elective Options Sociology, Psychology, Philosophy, Fine Arts, Music, Physical Education, Legal Studies, Computer Science. Choose what genuinely interests you — these electives can shape your college path.
  • Can You Take Maths in Arts? Yes. Applied Maths is available as an elective in many schools under the Humanities stream. It is different from Science Maths — more statistics-focused. Worth considering if you want options in social research or economics later.
👥 School vs. Coaching — The Real Story
Arts is the stream where school matters most and coaching is least critical. But here is what you should know:
  • Good teachers make an enormous difference in Humanities. A teacher who explains History through stories, not just dates, changes how you absorb the subject.
  • Answer-writing coaching is genuinely helpful. Many students know the content but score poorly because they don't structure answers. Practice with model answers from CBSE toppers.
  • NCERT textbooks for Humanities are exceptionally well-written. They are not just board-exam material — they are genuinely worth reading. Don't treat them like rote-learning targets.
  • If you want to crack CUET for top DU colleges, specific CUET coaching is helpful in Class 12. It is a separate exam from boards and needs separate preparation.
📊 Board Exam Pattern (CBSE)
  • Each subject is 80 marks written + 20 marks internal (projects, presentations, assignments). Total: 100 marks.
  • Questions range from 1-mark source-based to 6-mark long answers. Long answers need clear structure: introduction, body points, conclusion. Headings and subheadings help examiners — use them.
  • Internals and projects (20 marks) are extremely important. History and Geography projects are graded seriously. Research them properly — they also deepen your subject understanding.
  • Map work in Geography carries guaranteed marks. Practice all prescribed maps. This is one of the most reliable score boosters in the entire stream.
⏳ What a Typical Week Looks Like
Monday – Friday
School (6–7 hrs) → Self-study (1.5–2.5 hrs). Read chapters, not just notes. Write at least one full answer per day for practice. This builds the habit your exam depends on.
Saturday
Current affairs reading and revision of the week's topics. Humanities students benefit enormously from staying aware of what's happening in the world — it enriches every subject.
Sunday
Answer writing practice. Take one previous year question and write a full timed answer. Check against the marking scheme. This is the most high-leverage habit in this stream.
Exam Season
Focus on consolidation — not new reading. Revise key dates, facts, maps, and definitions. Write as many previous year answers as possible. Presentation wins marks here.
💡 Things That Actually Help
Read NCERT cover to cover — it is excellent Write one full answer every day Maps in Geography = guaranteed marks Read a newspaper — not just for exams Structure every long answer with headings Debate and MUN sharpen your thinking directly
⚠️ The Biggest Mistake Arts Students Make
Leaving answer-writing practice for the last few weeks before boards. In Humanities, writing is the skill — and like any skill, it needs months of practice to become fluent. Students who score 95%+ in this stream all have one thing in common: they write regularly, not just read.
