📖 After Class 10 — Before You Choose a Stream

Entrance Exams — Know Before You Choose

The gap between your Class 10 result and Class 11 admission is your most underused research window. Use it to understand what each stream's competitive exams actually look like — before you decide.

Before Choosing Your Stream

Know the roads before you pick one

Most students choose Science, Commerce, or Arts based on what their parents say, what their friends are doing, or what they vaguely think will "get them a job." Very few students spend the gap between Class 10 results and Class 11 admission actually researching the entrance exams and colleges that each stream leads to.
💡 Don't chase marks or money
In life, you should never run after marks or money as your primary goal — not in academics, not in career, not in relationships. Give your absolute best in whatever you choose to do. Marks and money will come to you on their own.
📖 Use this gap to actually research
You have a few weeks between your result and admission. This is rare free time with zero academic pressure. Sit with each stream's exams and colleges. Know what the path looks like — before committing to it.
ℹ️
This section covers entrance exams for each stream — one at a time. Careers in each field will be covered separately. For now, just understand the exam landscape.
🔬 Science Stream — PCM / PCB / PCMB
Engineering Colleges — National Level

JEE Mains

Joint Entrance Examination — Mains | Conducted by NTA (National Testing Agency)
🏛️ What admission does it give?

JEE Mains gives admission to B.Tech, B.Arch, and B.Planning courses across 105 institutes through JoSAA counselling:

NITs — 31 National Institutes of Technology
Fully government-funded and owned by the central government. Among the most prestigious engineering colleges in India after IITs.
IIITs — 26 Indian Institutes of Information Technology
Offer only IT, Computer Science & Electronics branches. 5 are fully govt-funded; 21 run on PPP model (Public-Private Partnership — jointly funded by central govt, state govt, and industry).
GFTIs — 47 Government Funded Technical Institutes
Technical institutes receiving central or state government funding. Wide variety of branches available across the country.
IIEST Shibpur — 1 Indian Institute of Engineering Science & Technology, Shibpur
One of the oldest technical institutes in Asia (est. 1856). A single independent institute in the JoSAA system.

IITs — National Level (Stage 2)

JEE Advanced

Joint Entrance Examination — Advanced | 2nd and final stage of the JEE system | Conducted by one of the 7 old IITs on rotation
🏛️ What admission does it give?

JEE Advanced is the gateway to all 23 IITs (Indian Institutes of Technology) — for courses including:

B.TechBachelor of Technology
B.S.Bachelor of Science
B.Archvia AAT (see below)
B.Tech + M.TechDual Degree — 5-year integrated programme
B.S. + M.S.Dual Degree — 5-year integrated programme
IITs = 23 institutes across India, including IIT Bombay, IIT Delhi, IIT Madras, IIT Kanpur, IIT Kharagpur, IIT Roorkee, IIT Guwahati, and 16 newer IITs. Each IIT conducts its own seat allocation through JoSAA counselling.
👤 Who can appear?
JEE Mains qualification is mandatory
Only the top 2,50,000 candidates who qualify JEE Mains (across all categories) are eligible to appear for JEE Advanced. You cannot directly attempt JEE Advanced.
Class 12 with PCM
Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics are mandatory in Class 12. No minimum percentage requirement (unlike JEE Mains which requires 75%), but must have appeared in all 5 subjects in Class 12.
Age limit
General / OBC-NCL candidates must be born on or after October 1 of the year that is 25 years before the exam year. SC / ST / PwD candidates get 5 years of relaxation (born on or after October 1, 30 years prior).

Marking scheme: Not fixed
Unlike JEE Mains, JEE Advanced does not have a fixed marking pattern. Each year's question paper may have different question types — single correct, multiple correct, numerical answer — each with their own marks and negative marking rules. The total marks across Paper 1 + Paper 2 add up to 360.

📐 AAT — Architecture Aptitude Test

Students who want to pursue B.Arch at an IIT must clear a separate test called AAT (Architecture Aptitude Test), conducted after JEE Advanced results. Only JEE Advanced qualified candidates can appear for AAT. It tests freehand drawing, geometric drawing, visual composition, and three-dimensional perception.

Central Universities — National Level

CUET (UG)

Common University Entrance Test | Conducted by NTA (National Testing Agency)
🎓 Target Courses for PCM Students

CUET opens doors to prestigious central universities (like Delhi University, BHU, JNU, etc.) and offers diverse options beyond just core engineering:

B.Tech / Int. B.Tech-M.Tech
Available in select central universities like DU (Cluster Innovation Centre) and BHU.
B.Sc (Hons) / B.Sc (Pass)
Excellent for core science research in Physics, Chemistry, or Mathematics.
B.C.A / B.Sc Computer Science
Top choices for software and IT careers outside of the traditional engineering route.
🔄 Cross-Stream Options
PCM students are fully eligible to pivot to non-science fields. You can easily target courses like B.A (Hons), B.Com (Hons), Law (BA LLB), or Mass Communication based on your CUET scores.
📝 Exam Structure & Marking Scheme
Exam Mode
CBT-mode (Computer-Based Test). *NTA may use a hybrid pen-paper mode for subjects with very high registration.
Subject Choice
Candidates can choose a maximum of 6 subjects, which includes a mix of Languages, Domain Subjects (like Physics, Chemistry, Maths), and the General Test.
Paper Sections
Section 1: Languages (e.g., English)
Section 2: Domain Specific (Core Class 12 PCM subjects)
Section 3: General Test (Maths, Aptitude, Current Affairs — highly recommended for cross-stream courses)
Marking Scheme
+5 marks for Correct Answer
-1 mark for Incorrect Answer
0 marks for Unattempted Questions

State Engineering Exams & Domicile Rules

All State Engineering Exams

State-wise Entrance Exams, Eligibility Criteria & Other-State Quotas
🌍 Other State Candidates (OMS) Rule Explained

Kya aap dusre state ka engineering exam de sakte hain? Haan, bilkul de sakte hain! Lekin iske rules government aur private colleges ke liye alag hote hain:

🏛️ Government Colleges Rule
Lagbhag har state ke top Government Engineering Colleges (jaise COEP Pune, Jadavpur University) mein 85% to 100% seats sirf usi state ke local students (Domicile) ke liye hoti hain. Other state ke bacche inme aamtaur par eligible nahi hote ya sirf 5% to 15% seats (All India Quota through JEE Main) par hi fight kar sakte hain.
🏢 Private Colleges Rule
State ke saare top Private Engineering Colleges (jaise RVCE Bangalore, JSS Noida) mein doosre states ke bacchon ke liye badi window khuli hoti hai. Inke paas 15% to 20% dedicated All-India Quota / Management Quota seats hoti hain jahan aap direct ya state counselling ke through admission le sakte hain.
