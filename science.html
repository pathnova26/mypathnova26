Class 9 & 10

Know Your Stream First

🧬 What You Will Study
Physics, Chemistry, Biology (PCB) or Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics (PCM) — or all four (PCMB). Each subject demands a completely different way of thinking. Physics is about principles and applying them. Chemistry is part memory, part logic. Maths is pure problem-solving. Biology is detailed understanding of living systems.
⏱️ How Much You Need to Study
Expect 4–7 hours of self-study daily in Class 11–12, on top of school hours. Concepts stack on top of each other — If you miss one week, the next week becomes twice as hard.
📖 The Big Difference from Class 10
Class 10 Science is about understanding the world around you. Class 11 Science is about understanding why it works — through mathematics, derivations, and proofs. Rote learning stops working entirely.
💡 What Kind of Student Thrives Here
Genuinely curious about how things work Enjoys solving problems step-by-step Comfortable with long study hours Prefers understanding over memorizing
⚠️ Honest Reality
Science is a strong choice if you love the subjects — not just because of parental pressure or because you scored 90%+ in Class 10. Many students who topped Class 10 struggle in Science because they relied on memorization, not understanding.
☆ Opportunities Students Should Know Early

These are not mandatory — but students who do them early build a profile that opens doors others don't even know exist.

🛡️ NCC (National Cadet Corps)
If you're interested in Defence, ISRO, or government research roles, NCC builds your profile significantly. The A/B/C certificates are recognized in many university admissions. It builds discipline and time management — which are non-negotiable in Science.
Defence profile Admission advantage Leadership
🏆 Regional / National Representation
Representing your school or district in Science exhibitions, Maths olympiads, or even sports shows that you perform under pressure. Universities — especially engineering and medical colleges — value consistent achievers outside the classroom.
Student profile Scholarship eligibility Interview edge
🗣️ Debate / MUN / Public Speaking
Many Science students ignore this. That is a mistake. Soft skills matter enormously in research interviews, college selections, and jobs. If you can explain a complex concept clearly, you stand apart from thousands who just solved the same problems.
Communication skills Interview confidence College selections
🔬 Olympiads & Science Competitions
NTSE, NSO, KVPY (now IQMR), Olympiads in Physics, Chemistry, Maths, Biology — these build your competitive mindset early. They appear on college applications and can open scholarship doors. Even preparing trains you for JEE/NEET thinking.
Scholarships IIT/NIT profile STEM exposure
code/ Coding / Projects
If you are PCM, learning to code — even basic Python or C++ — while in Class 9-10 puts you years ahead. Tech colleges love students with project portfolios. Build something small: a calculator, a game, a website. It is more impressive than any certificate.

Class 11 & 12

What These Two Years Actually Look Like

💎 Subject Combinations
You will choose one of these combinations depending on your goal:
  • PCM — Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics For engineering, tech, architecture, data science. Maths is the backbone of everything here.
  • PCB — Physics, Chemistry, Biology For medicine, dentistry, pharmacy, nursing, biotech. Biology becomes very detailed and memory-intensive.
  • PCMB — All four subjects Gives maximum flexibility but maximum load. Only recommended if you are genuinely strong in all areas.
👥 School vs. Coaching — The Real Story
Most serious Science students attend coaching classes (Kota model, local coaching, or online platforms like PW, Unacademy, Allen). Here is what nobody tells you:
  • School covers the NCERT syllabus. Coaching covers the competitive exam syllabus. These overlap but are not identical — you need both.
  • Many students attend coaching 4–5 days a week after school. This means studying from 7am to 10pm on most days. This is the reality — plan for it.
  • Online coaching has made quality teaching accessible. You do not have to go to Kota anymore. But you do need self-discipline — nobody tracks you online.
  • NCERT is non-negotiable for boards. Never ignore it chasing coaching material.
📊 Board Exam Pattern (CBSE)
  • Each subject is 70 marks written + 30 marks practical/internal. Total: 100 marks.
  • Questions range from MCQs (1 mark) to long derivations and numericals (5 marks). Speed and accuracy both matter.
  • Practicals in Physics, Chemistry, Biology are real lab work — graded by an external examiner. They are easier to score in, but you must actually prepare.
  • Class 12 board marks matter for: college cutoffs, CUET (for some programs), and scholarship eligibility — not just entrance rank.
⏳ What a Typical Week Looks Like

This is not a motivational quote. This is the actual schedule most serious Science students follow:

Monday – Friday
School (6-7 hrs) → Coaching (2-3 hrs) → Self-study / revision (1-2 hrs). Sleep by 11pm if you're disciplined.
Saturday
Full coaching day or weekly mock test. Review your mistakes in the evening — this is where the real learning happens.
Sunday
Catch-up on weak topics. Revise the week's content. One subject deep-dive. Rest is important — don't sacrifice it entirely.
Exam Season
Intensity doubles. Previous year papers, timed practice sets, and formula revision every morning. Consistency beats last-minute cramming every time.
💡 Things That Actually Help
Solve previous year papers from Class 11 itself NCERT first, coaching material second Weekly revision beats daily cramming One subject strong = confidence anchor Never skip practicals — easy marks Study groups work only if everyone is serious
⚠️ The Class 11 Warning
Class 11 is where most students fall behind — and never fully recover in Class 12. The syllabus is new, the pace is fast, and the jump from Class 10 feels like a wall. Do not take the first few months lightly just because "it doesn't count for boards." It counts for everything that comes after.
